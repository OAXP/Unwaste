Gleaning ?

Gleaning is defined as the harvesting of leftover crops from a farmer's field after the commercial harvest. Often, the crop has already been harvested and simply needs to be picked up. Gleaning is an ancient practice that is being revived in urban and rural settings, on public and private land, and most often includes fruits, nuts and/or vegetables. Gleaning is a way for individuals to participate in the process of obtaining food in a dignified manner. Join us and help Quebec farms reduce their crop loses at the end of the season or even before!

FARMERS

Farmers who are willing to donate their surplus crops are absolutely absolutely essential to a gleaning initiative. The feeling of having contributed to the community is perhaps the greatest motivation for farmers to donate their surplus crops.

The possible benefits:

- crops left in the field may attract pests or disease,

- farmers are happy that the food is going to a good cause and not wasted

- increased community awareness of farming practices
